2. Identifying Volcano Rocks

Intrusive vs Extrusive Texture

Extrusive Rocks will typicaly have much larger crystal formations while Extrusive rocks you will not be able to see the crystals or they will be very small.

Rock Composition

Rocks with higher iron will be a darker color and rocks with high silica will be a light color. Additionaly rocks that are more grey will be about equal or intermediate.

Using Texture and Composition

using both of these techniques allows us to classify rock within a range of different types.

3. Identifying Volcano Hazards

Lava Flows

Lava flows occur when magma from below earths crust escapes to the surface and runs down slopes. Most lava flows move slowly and are a result of volcanic activity. The speed and distance of a lava flow depend on how thick or runny the lava is.

Pyroclastic Flows

Pyroclastic flows are fast-moving clouds of extremely hot gas, ash, and volcanic rock that rush down the sides of a volcano during explosive eruptions. These flows can travel very fast and dont offer much evacuation time. Thankfully they usualy dont get very far from the volcano. Because of their speed and heat, pyroclastic flows are one of the most dangerous volcanic hazards.

Lahars

Lahars are volcanic mudflows made of water, ash, rock, and debris that flow down river valleys and slopes near a volcano. They can be triggered by heavy rain, melting snow, or volcanic eruptions. Lahars behave like flowing concrete and can travel long distances, burying towns and damaging structures far from the volcano.

Ash Clouds

Volcanic ash clouds form when explosive eruptions send tiny fragments of rock and glass high into the atmosphere. Falling ash can damage buildings, contaminate water supplies, harm lungs, and disrupt transportation. Ash clouds are especially dangerous to aircraft because ash particles can damage jet engines.

Volcanic Gasses

Volcanoes release gases such as water vapor, carbon dioxide, and sulfur dioxide during eruptions. Some volcanic gases can be harmful or deadly when concentrated, especially in low-lying areas. These gases can also cause acid rain and contribute to air pollution that affects people, animals, and plants.

Climate Effects

Large volcanic eruptions can affect Earth's climate by releasing ash and sulfur-rich gases into the upper atmosphere. These particles reflect sunlight back into space, which can lower global temperatures for months or even years. Historic eruptions have caused temporary cooling and changes in weather patterns around the world.

4. What to Do When Visiting a Volcano

Before Visiting

  • Check the volcanoâ€™s current activity and alert level with local authorities.
  • Wear sturdy shoes, long pants, and weather-appropriate clothing for rugged terrain.
  • Bring essential supplies such as water, snacks, sunscreen, a hat, and a map of the area.

While Visiting

  • Stay on marked trails and follow all safety signs and instructions from guides.
  • Keep a safe distance from lava flows, fumaroles, and unstable slopes.
  • Wear a mask or cover your nose and mouth if ash or volcanic dust is present.
